Mr. Scatman John

The man who effortlessly blended jazz, scat, and infectious beats

Mr. Scatman John takes the jacket photo for his new album.

Here's a time line of Mr. Scatman John's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Toledo, Ohio, John Paul Larkin came into the world during the challenging times of World War II in the United States. Growing up in a humble household, he was captivated by the allure of music from a young age.
  • 1950s ~ 1960s - Immersed in the beauty of jazz, scat, and piano during his youth, Scatman John, as he would later be known, faced the struggles of a language disorder, making verbal expression a daily challenge.
  • 1970s - Commencing his journey to Canada, he began walking the path of music in Toronto. While refining his skills in piano and scat, a protracted battle with language-related issues began.
  • Early 1980 - As he aspired to succeed as a music producer, economic hardships struck. Paradoxically, this adversity spurred his creativity, leading him to explore new musical directions.
  • 1986 - Migrating to Sweden, he not only pursued music production but also innovatively embraced scat as a means to confront his own language disorder, marking a turning point in his career.
  • 1991 - Determined to face his language disorder head-on, he decided to wield scat as a tool for self-expression through music. This decision became a pivotal moment in his career.
  • 1994 - With the release of his debut album "Scatman's World," featuring advanced scat techniques and optimistic lyrics, he gained widespread attention, stepping into the spotlight.
  • 1995 - Scatman (Ski-Ba-Bop-Ba-Dop-Bop)" became a global hit, propelling Scatman John into international stardom. His music resonated with people worldwide, bringing hope and energy.
  • 1996 - The album "Everybody Jam!" achieved success, solidifying Scatman John's position as an iconic figure overcoming language barriers in the music industry.
  • 1998 - Unveiling the album "Take Your Time," the track "Scatman's Dance" garnered widespread acclaim.
  • 1999 - Putting an end to his battle with language disorders, he passed away at the age of 57. Despite his departure, his music and message endure, continuing to inspire countless individuals.
  • 2000s - Scatman John's music found its way into movies and advertisements, becoming widely recognized by new generations. His timeless tunes continued to be cherished.
  • 2010s - Experiencing a reevaluation on the internet, his optimistic messages gained renewed attention. His influence expanded, appealing to a new fan base.
  • 2015 - March 13, his birthday, became "Scatman Day," internationally celebrated in recognition of his achievements.
  • 2020s - Scatman John's music continues to be used in diverse media, and the difficulties he overcame and his optimistic message still provide courage and hope to many.

“My greatest problem in my childhood is now my greatest asset. I’m trying to tell the kids today that Creation gave us all problems for a purpose, and that your biggest problems contain a source of strength to not only step over those problems, but all our other problems as well.”

--- by Mr. Scatman John
